Tips & Safety

Beat
coasterphobia.

Practical advice from riders who've been where you are. Knowledge is the most powerful fear-killer there is.

01
Start with low intensity
Dark indoor rides and family coasters are the perfect entry point. You can't see drops coming — and that actually helps. Build a win before going bigger.
02
Choose your seat wisely
Front = maximum visibility. Back = maximum airtime. Middle = the balanced experience. For beginners, sit in the middle rows — neither extreme.
03
Breathe through drops
Exhale on the way down. Your body tenses naturally — releasing breath signals your brain that you're safe. Practiced riders swear by this every time.
04
Watch riders exit
Stand near the exit and watch others come off smiling. Their body language will calm your nervous system before you even board. It's basic psychology.
05
Use the AI advisor
Ask our chatbot anything before you board — height, sensation, duration, what a drop feels like. Knowledge kills the unknown, and the unknown is what scares us.
06
Safety first, always
All accredited parks run full mechanical inspections before opening every day. Restraints, track, and electronics checked daily. You are safer than you feel.
